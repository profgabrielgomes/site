Gabriel Gomes

PhD student in Mathematics at CIMAT

Short Bio

Hi! I'm Gabriel, a PhD student in Mathematics at CIMAT (Centro de Investigación en Matemáticas) in Mexico, under the supervision of Professor Héctor Andrés Chang Lara and Professor Jimmy Petean. My research focuses on the analysis of partial differential equations, particularly on foundational results in the theory of viscosity solutions.

Recently, I have concentrated on the study of stable solutions to elliptic partial differential equations, as well as the uniqueness and stability of saddle-shaped solutions to the Allen–Cahn equation.

Before joining CIMAT, I completed my Master's degree in Mathematics at PUC-Rio (Pontifícia Universidade Católica do Rio de Janeiro) in Brasil under the supervision of Professor Boyan Sirakov and Professora Pammella Queiroz, where I developed a strong interest in differential equations.

You can check my CV and my Lattes CV.

Research

My research focuses on the analysis of partial differential equations (PDEs) and the theory of viscosity solutions. I am particularly interested in understanding the existence, uniqueness, and stability of solutions to elliptic PDEs.

Recently, I have been studying:

  • Stable solutions to elliptic partial differential equations
  • Uniqueness and stability of saddle-shaped solutions to the Allen–Cahn equation
  • Regularity theory for viscosity solutions

I am always open to discussing new ideas and collaborations in these areas — feel free to reach out!
